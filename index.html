Abstract

Foley sound synthesis, as framed by the DCASE 2023 Task 7 benchmark, aims to generate sound effects conditioned on a scene category, and is inherently a multi-modal, one-to-many problem in which each category admits many acoustically valid realizations while only a few hours of labeled audio are available for training. Existing systems for this benchmark rely on discrete autoregressive priors or iterative diffusion denoising; both can be suboptimal under such small and multi-modal conditions, where preserving sample diversity while maintaining scene fidelity is the central difficulty. We propose a mel-domain Foley synthesis framework based on Schrödinger Bridge Conditional Flow Matching (SB-CFM). Unlike diffusion models that reverse an iterative denoising process, SB-CFM directly learns a velocity field along an optimal-transport-coupled Brownian-bridge path whose time-dependent noise preserves trajectory diversity. A two-dimensional U-Net with zero-initialized adaptive group-normalization conditioning captures scene identity, while the frame-level root-mean-square envelope of the target, injected through block-wise feature-wise modulation, provides explicit temporal control. We further introduce a simple stochastic sampler that perturbs the deterministic update with noise scaled to the training bridge and, combined with classifier-free guidance, balances conditional fidelity and diversity. On the DCASE 2023 Task 7 dataset we evaluate with six complementary metrics covering distributional similarity, conditioning fidelity, diversity, semantic alignment, and temporal fidelity. SB-CFM attains a Fréchet Audio Distance of 2.57, a 46.0% reduction relative to the strongest baseline, along with the lowest Kernel Audio Distance and the lowest temporal error. An automatic neural proxy for mean opinion score further corroborates its perceptual quality.

§ III — Proposed method

A stochastic bridge in the mel domain, steered by an envelope

We generate in the log-mel domain. A standard-Gaussian source is coupled to a target spectrogram by an exact intra-class optimal-transport assignment, and the two are joined by a Schrödinger-bridge path: a straight line plus a Brownian-bridge perturbation whose variance vanishes at both endpoints and peaks at t = ½, so the marginals match exactly while every path interior stays randomized. A 2-D U-Net regresses the closed-form velocity — scene identity enters through zero-initialized adaptive group normalization, and the frame-level RMS envelope enters through block-wise FiLM, which is what controls when energy appears. At inference a 50-step Euler update, perturbed with noise scaled to the training bridge and guided per scene, is decoded by the official DCASE HiFi-GAN vocoder — the same one every baseline uses. Setting σ = 0 recovers OT-CFM exactly, which is why the ablations below can vary the bridge on its own.

§ V — Results

Six metrics, because no single one covers fidelity, diversity, and timing

All numbers come from a single training run without seed averaging, so small differences should be read with care. Two caveats bound comparability: the AudioLDM checkpoint we run is the general-purpose model, not the challenge entry built on it, and our guidance scale is tuned per scene on the development split while the baselines run at their published defaults.

Limitations. Moving motor vehicle remains far from the original recordings for every system, suggesting quasi-stationary textures need dedicated treatment. We regress only the velocity field and not the score, so the model recovers the marginal path of the bridge rather than the bridge process, and the stochastic sampler is correspondingly an empirical device. All results come from a single training run without seed averaging. This work relies on objective and automatic perceptual metrics rather than human listening tests.
