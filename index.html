How it works

A stochastic bridge in the mel domain, steered by an RMS envelope

Foley is a one-to-many problem on a small corpus: seven labels, about 5.4 hours of audio, and each label covers many acoustically valid sounds. Our system keeps that fan-out instead of averaging it away.

Probability path

Schrödinger bridge, not a denoising chain

We interpolate linearly between a standard-Gaussian source and a target log-mel spectrogram, then add a Brownian-bridge perturbation whose variance vanishes at both endpoints and peaks at t = ½. The marginals stay matched exactly while the interior of every path is randomized, so a single label can still fan out into many valid sounds. The network regresses the closed-form velocity — training is simulation-free mean-squared regression, with no score to estimate.

Coupling

Intra-class optimal transport

Each mini-batch is coupled with an exact Hungarian assignment, restricted to run within each scene class so class identity is preserved and the O(B³) solve breaks into per-class blocks. Short, non-crossing paths mean the learned velocity field stays close to straight, which is what buys the low step count.

Conditioning

RMS envelope as a first-class signal

Scene identity enters every residual block through zero-initialized adaptive group normalization. The frame-level RMS envelope of the target enters separately: block-wise FiLM in the decoder pools it into 32 blocks and predicts a per-block scale and shift, and a lightweight zero-init 1-D convolution adds channel-wise modulation in the encoder. This is what gives explicit control over when energy appears — and removing it is the single most damaging ablation.

Sampling

Stochastic Euler update + per-scene guidance

The deterministic Euler step is perturbed with isotropic noise scaled to the training bridge (σ_stoch = ησ√2, η = 0.25), with no noise on the final step so the terminal marginal is unperturbed. Classifier-free guidance trades diversity against fidelity, and the scale is chosen per scene. Both samplers cost the same two network passes per step.

Velocity network

2-D U-Net over log-mel

80 mel bands × 345 frames from four seconds at 22.05 kHz. Base width 64, multipliers [1, 2, 4, 4], three residual blocks per level, 2-D self-attention at the two deepest levels and the bottleneck. The output convolution is zero-initialized, so the flow initially leaves x₀ unchanged.

Training

1000 epochs, one uninterrupted run

AdamW (lr 1.4e−4, weight decay 1e−6), cosine schedule with 1000 warmup steps, dropout 0.15, EMA 0.999, bf16, global batch 48 on two GPUs, class-balanced batches. Label dropout p = 0.15 for guidance; the RMS envelope is never dropped. Per-class weights up-weight gunshot (3), sneeze/cough (2), and moving motor vehicle (1.5).

Audio samples

Three clips per scene, same envelope across every system

Every clip is four seconds, monophonic, 22.05 kHz, and decoded with the same official DCASE HiFi-GAN vocoder — no system gains a vocoder advantage. Within a column, all temporally conditioned systems were given the same RMS envelope, taken from the original recording in that column.

What you hear, seen

Mel spectrograms, and the envelope they were asked to follow

The differences the metrics report are visible. Baselines smear the sharp attacks that define gunshot and footstep; our transport paths keep onsets and harmonic structure intact. Moving motor vehicle is the hardest category for every system, and the row shows it.

Quantitative results

Six metrics, because no single one covers fidelity, diversity, and timing

All numbers come from a single training run without seed averaging, so small differences should be read with care. Two caveats bound comparability: the AudioLDM checkpoint is the general-purpose model rather than the challenge entry built on it, and our guidance scale is tuned per scene on the development split while baselines run at their published defaults.
